Energy Retrofit Master Plan

Strategic pathway to transform a 1960 detached home from F-rated performance to A-range efficiency through fabric upgrades, heat pump readiness, and integrated renewable energy.

Current BER: F | Target: A Range | Location: Kilkenny

Executive Summary

This project aims to maximise the energy performance of the property by reducing the Heat Loss Indicator (HLI), transitioning to a heat pump system, upgrading legacy glazing, and integrating solar PV. The standard advisory pathway projects a B1 rating; this plan seeks to exceed that baseline and position the dwelling for A-level performance.

Current BER

F

Primary Energy

414.97 kWh/m²/yr

Current HLI

5.344 W/(K·m²)

Advisory Potential

Strategic Objective

  • Reduce HLI below 2.0 for optimal heat pump efficiency
  • Replace 40-year-old glazing with high-performance units
  • Upgrade insulation beyond minimum thresholds where viable
  • Install air-to-water heat pump
  • Add solar PV to push final BER into A range
Primary Goal: Achieve heat pump readiness through deep fabric improvement and maximise Renewable Energy Ratio via solar integration.

Priority Discussion Points for Assessor

4. Solar PV Pathway
