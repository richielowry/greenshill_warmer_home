1950s Detached – 121m² – Current BER: F – Gas Heating

Current Situation

  • 1950s detached property (high exposed surface area)
  • Cavity walls (previously filled)
  • Gas boiler heating
  • F BER rating (significant heat loss)
Major improvement potential. Large gains achievable through insulation + heating upgrade.

Primary Objectives

  • Maximise fully funded upgrade measures
  • Make property heat-pump ready
  • Target B1/B2 rating initially
  • Plan staged path toward A rating

Key Measures to Push With Assessor

1. Attic Insulation

  • Upgrade to minimum 300mm+
  • Ensure full coverage and thermal bridging reduction

2. External Wall Insulation (Critical)

  • Evaluate suitability despite cavity fill
  • Highest remaining BER improvement potential

3. Airtightness & Ventilation

  • Comprehensive draught proofing
  • Demand-controlled or mechanical ventilation if required

4. Heat Pump Assessment

  • Confirm heat loss indicator after insulation
  • Determine eligibility for gas boiler replacement
Direct Question to Ask: “What is required to make this house heat-pump ready?”

Expected BER Outcomes

  • Insulation only → C1 to B3 range
  • Insulation + Heat Pump → B1 / Possible A3
  • Add Solar PV → Likely A rating

Strategic Upgrade Plan

  1. Accept all fully funded insulation upgrades
  2. Ensure heat pump qualification review
  3. Obtain updated BER after works
  4. Add solar PV (if needed) to achieve A rating
