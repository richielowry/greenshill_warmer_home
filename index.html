Home Energy Upgrade Project

1950s Detached | 121m² | Current BER: F | Gas Heating

Overall Project Goal

Achieve the highest possible BER rating by making the house fully heat-pump ready, integrating solar PV generation, and upgrading thermal performance (including 40-year-old windows).

Primary objective: Transition from F rating to B1/A range through staged, strategic upgrades.

Strategic Priorities

  1. Reduce heat loss through insulation and airtightness.
  2. Make property compliant for heat pump installation.
  3. Replace outdated windows (40 years old) to improve efficiency.
  4. Integrate solar PV to maximise final BER rating.

Key Requests for Assessor

1. Heat Pump Readiness Assessment (Critical)

  • Confirm required heat loss indicator (HLI) threshold.
  • Identify insulation upgrades necessary to qualify.
  • Evaluate suitability for gas boiler replacement.
Direct Question: “What must be completed to make this house fully heat-pump ready?”

2. Attic Insulation Upgrade

  • Increase to minimum 300mm+
  • Ensure complete coverage and reduced thermal bridging

3. External Wall Insulation Review

  • Evaluate despite previous cavity fill
  • Assess BER impact and cost-effectiveness

4. Window Replacement

  • Replace 40-year-old glazing with high-performance double/triple glazing
  • Improve airtightness and reduce draughts
  • Confirm BER impact of window upgrade

5. Airtightness & Ventilation

  • Draught proofing measures
  • Ventilation upgrades to support heat pump efficiency

6. Solar PV Integration

  • Confirm roof suitability (orientation & shading)
  • Determine PV system size needed to achieve A rating

Projected BER Pathway

  • Insulation Only: F → C1/B3
  • Insulation + Heat Pump: Likely B1/B2
  • Add Solar PV: Potential A3/A2

Implementation Plan

  1. Complete all funded insulation upgrades.
  2. Achieve heat pump qualification threshold.
  3. Install heat pump system.
  4. Replace windows.
  5. Add solar PV to maximise final BER.
